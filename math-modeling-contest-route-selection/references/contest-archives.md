# Contest Archives And Historical Scope

Use this file when the user asks for historical grounding, long-horizon distillation, or official archive links.

## Official Archive Anchors

- CUMCM official historical problem pages are available from the China Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics / National Undergraduate Mathematical Contest in Modeling site. The public archive includes annual CUMCM problem statements from 1992 onward, with recent years such as 2025 listed on the same official site.
- COMAP's MCM/ICM contest matrix provides an official year-by-year matrix of MCM and ICM problems from 1985 through 2026.

Do not claim to have exhaustively read every problem unless the current task actually loads or processes every file. Phrase the distilled view as "based on the official archive structure and recurring contest archetypes."

## What The Long-Horizon Pattern Shows

Across CUMCM, MCM, and ICM, winning-level topic selection usually favors a team that can combine:

- A precise real-world decision object.
- A mathematical core that fits the question rather than a fashionable algorithm.
- A credible data or parameter plan.
- A validation strategy visible inside the paper.
- A distinct narrative: "our model sees something the baseline misses."

## Recurring Contest Evolution

1. Early modeling style: mechanism equations, operations research, physics and engineering systems, deterministic optimization, and simplified assumptions.
2. Data-rich expansion: forecasting, statistics, image/data processing, ranking/evaluation, and multi-source data fusion.
3. Complex systems phase: networks, epidemics, environment, logistics, resource allocation, agent behavior, robustness, policy simulation.
4. AI-assisted era: common AI outputs make method names less valuable; differentiation shifts toward feature design, validation, interpretability, scenario construction, and the way results are argued.

## Historical Use Guidance

When using history:

- Map current problems to archetypes, not to old answer templates.
- Borrow validation patterns more than exact methods.
- Look for what a generic answer will overuse, then choose a defensible deviation.
- Prefer "simple baseline + justified upgrade" over "stack of advanced models."
